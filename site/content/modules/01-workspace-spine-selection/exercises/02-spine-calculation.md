## What you'll do

Work through the complete spine selection calculation for the matched set using two tools — the Rose City Archery {{< gloss "Static spine" "static spine" >}} chart and Stu Miller's {{< gloss "Dynamic spine" "dynamic spine" >}} calculator — record where they agree and where they diverge, apply the per-inch draw-length adjustment rule to produce a personalized spine target, and write a finished spec card that serves as your purchase reference for module 2.

## Setup

You need: a browser open to the Rose City Archery spine chart at [rosecityarchery.com/pages/spine-weight-recurve-longbow-compound-bow-chart](https://www.rosecityarchery.com/pages/spine-weight-recurve-longbow-compound-bow-chart) and to Stu Miller's calculator at [heilakka.com/stumiller](https://heilakka.com/stumiller/). A pencil and the spec card template below. No tools required.

Starting parameters for this build: **40 lb bow, 28-inch AMO {{< gloss "Draw length" "draw length" >}} (working default), 100-grain {{< gloss "Glue-on field point" "glue-on field point" >}}, 11/32-inch {{< gloss "Port Orford cedar" "Port Orford cedar shaft" >}}.**

## Verification

You've completed this exercise correctly when:

1. You have two recorded spine numbers (chart and calculator) that are within approximately 5–10# of each other.
2. You have a personalized spine target adjusted for your actual or default draw length.
3. Every field on the spec card is filled in.
4. You can explain — in one sentence — why a stiff arrow kicks one direction and a weak arrow kicks the other for a left-handed shooter.

Staple or tape the spec card to your bench next to your workspace-ready sticky note from Exercise 1. These two documents together are the green light to place your shaft order.
